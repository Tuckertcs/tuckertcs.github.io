| How to Make a Custom Textured Rubik's Cube

| Materials

  • ❱ Rubik's Cube
  • ❱ Scissors
  • ❱ Textures
  • ❱ Super Glue
  • ❱ Ruler

You can buy many of these items on Amazon, you can also buy art supplies like the textures at Michaels.

| STEP 1 : Collect Six Unique Textures

Make sure these textures are very unique so you can feel the difference without looking and be able to solve with your eyes closed. Sand paper, velcro, foam, and similar are all good texture ideas. Here are some textures you can buy at Amazon:

  • ❱ Velcro (counts as two sides!)
  • ❱ Sand Paper
  • ❱ Rubber Trailer Grip
  • ❱ Flat Lego Tiles
  • ❱ No Texture (remember that smooth plastic is a texture!)

| STEP 2 : Removing Stickers

Now use a thin object like a knife or your finger nails to carefully remove the stickers from your cube. If there is any residue left on the cube, wash it off and let the cube dry.

| STEP 3 : Applying New ''Stickers''

Some of your textures might have sticky backs. If they do, cut them into 9 squares and stick them onto your cube. For things that don't have sticky backs, like the lego tiles, use super glue to put them on. Once you do that, you're done! Now try solving it with your eyes closed!
